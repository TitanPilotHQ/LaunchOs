# Titan Pilot Brand Bible v1

## Brand Role

Titan Pilot is not a signal seller, retail trading bot, or returns-marketing company. It is the accountability layer for AI-assisted trading decisions.

## Category

**Supervised AI Trading**

## Positioning

**The system of record for AI trading decisions.**

Titan Pilot turns every AI thesis, objection, score, approval, refusal, and execution consequence into machine-validated, replayable evidence.

## Brand Promise

Every important decision should be:

- explainable
- cited
- challenged
- deterministic where possible
- replayable
- auditable
- governed by a human or explicit policy

## Brand Story

Trading firms are already using AI. Most of that usage is invisible: private prompts, screenshots, undocumented overrides, and no durable record of what the model said or why anyone acted on it.

Titan Pilot exists to make that behavior governable. It records the full decision chain—from market evidence through AI reasoning, adversarial review, deterministic scoring, operator approval, and final outcome—so a desk can prove what happened without reconstructing it from memory.

## Visual Principles

- the unit of the interface is the decision, not the chart
- evidence receives more visual weight than decoration
- model output is visually distinct from deterministic data
- inaction is rendered as competence
- critical red is reserved for HALTED or genuine integrity failure
- numbers never animate like casino counters
- no bulls, bears, luxury imagery, cash piles, or humanoid trading robots

## Typography

- UI: Inter or system sans
- Numerics: IBM Plex Mono or Geist Mono with tabular figures
- Marketing headlines: restrained, no oversized hype typography
- Prices always use full market precision

## Logo Usage

- maintain generous clear space
- do not place over noisy charts
- do not add gradients, shadows, bevels, or animated flames
- dark and light variants must preserve contrast
- the symbol may appear alone only where Titan Pilot is already established

## Content Hierarchy

1. Problem
2. Evidence
3. Control
4. Operational proof
5. Qualified call to action

Never lead with feature volume.

## Public Claims Standard

Every measurable claim requires:

- source document
- evidence date
- public-safe wording
- owner
- expiry/review date

Unsupported claims do not ship.

## Brand Red Lines

Titan Pilot must never:

- imply guaranteed or expected investment returns
- hide failed AI outputs
- treat skipped trades as lost opportunities
- use retail gambling language
- create urgency around approvals
- encourage overtrading
- confuse demo evidence with customer production evidence

## Definition of Done

A Titan Pilot asset is on-brand when a professional trading-desk buyer can understand, within seconds:

1. this is not a signal-selling product
2. the product governs AI decisions
3. evidence is the primary differentiator
4. the system can refuse action
5. every public claim can be verified
