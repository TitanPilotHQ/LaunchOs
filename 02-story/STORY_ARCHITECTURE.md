# Titan Pilot Story Architecture

## Category story
Trading desks already use AI, but most cannot prove what it told them, what evidence it used, who challenged it, who approved it, or what it cost. Titan Pilot turns those invisible AI whispers into durable evidence.

## Founder story
Titan Pilot was built in the reverse order of most AI products. The event spine, replay proof, broker reconciliation, recovery, verification, backups, and operating discipline came before the first production LLM call. The sequence is the proof of the philosophy: infrastructure before intelligence, evidence before autonomy.

## Customer story
### Before
- Traders use private AI workflows.
- Analysis lives in screenshots and chat history.
- API spend is invisible.
- Risk committees receive policy documents rather than system evidence.
- Incidents cannot be reconstructed.

### After
- One sanctioned AI decision pipeline.
- Every model claim cites stored evidence.
- A Devil's Advocate challenges the thesis.
- Deterministic software calculates the score.
- Human approvals and refusals are attributable.
- Every decision can be replayed.

## Product story
The product is not the trade. The product is the decision record: dossier, thesis, objections, deterministic score, governance action, execution result, and outcome.

## Investor story
The beachhead is professional FX/CFD desks. The category is Supervised AI Trading. The compounding asset is the evidence corpus, open decision-evidence schema, and certification process.

## Public demo story
1. Show the ungoverned-AI problem.
2. Show a fabricated citation caught by the validator.
3. Show a Devil's Advocate veto.
4. Expand the deterministic score.
5. Run the replay proof.
6. End with the paid AI Desk Audit.

## Narrative red lines
Never sell returns. Never imply the product eliminates human responsibility. Never present shadow outcomes as live performance. Never hide a system refusal or validator rejection.

## Definition of done
A story is approved when the buyer can retell it accurately in one minute, the risk officer finds no unsupported claim, and every proof point links to evidence.
