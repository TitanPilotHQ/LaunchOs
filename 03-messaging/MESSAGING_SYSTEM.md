# Titan Pilot Messaging System

## Purpose

Provide one approved language system for the website, sales calls, investor materials, demos, social content, and partnerships.

## Category Statement

Titan Pilot defines **Supervised AI Trading**: AI-assisted trading in which every model claim is linked to evidence, challenged, scored by deterministic software, governed by explicit controls, and preserved as a replayable decision record.

## One-Sentence Positioning

Titan Pilot is the system of record for AI trading decisions.

## 10-Second Pitch

Your traders already use AI. Titan Pilot turns every thesis, objection, approval, and refusal into evidence your desk can replay and defend.

## 30-Second Pitch

Trading desks are adopting AI faster than their risk controls can keep up. Most AI-assisted decisions live in browser tabs and screenshots with no reliable record of what the model saw, what it claimed, who approved it, or why the desk acted. Titan Pilot provides a supervised decision pipeline with machine-validated citations, adversarial review, deterministic scoring, human approval, hard budget controls, and replayable audit trails.

## Two-Minute Pitch

Titan Pilot does not sell trading signals or promise alpha. It governs the AI a professional desk is already using. Market context is converted into a structured dossier. An AI analyst produces a cited thesis. A second agent challenges it. Software validates every citation and calculates the score using versioned deterministic rules. The decision is then skipped, vetoed, proposed for human approval, or—only through the existing risk and execution controls—allowed to proceed. Every step is stored as an immutable event and can be reconstructed later.

The result is a glass-box operating record for AI-assisted trading: what the model said, why it said it, what disagreed, what the controls decided, what the human approved, what it cost, and what happened next.

## Objection Responses

### “Where are the returns?”

Titan Pilot does not sell returns. It governs the process by which a desk uses AI. Alpha remains the desk’s responsibility; decision accountability is ours.

### “Why pay for shadow mode?”

Because shadow mode creates the evidence record without adding execution liability. It shows whether the AI process is useful, compliant, and economically controlled before authority expands.

### “We already use ChatGPT.”

That is the problem Titan Pilot governs. A browser transcript is not a decision system, risk control, approval trail, or durable audit record.

### “Our traders will feel monitored.”

Titan Pilot primarily monitors the AI’s claims and the decision process. The record can also protect traders by proving when they rejected or overrode the model.

### “Couldn’t we build this?”

Yes, with enough time. The relevant question is whether rebuilding event sourcing, validation, replay, budget controls, approval provenance, and operational certification is the desk’s best use of engineering capacity.
