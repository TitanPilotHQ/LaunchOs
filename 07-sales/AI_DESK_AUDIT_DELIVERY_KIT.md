# AI Desk Audit — Delivery Kit

## Offer

A paid, fixed-scope two-week assessment of how a professional trading desk currently uses AI, measured against the TITAN Decision Evidence Standard.

The audit does not provide trading advice, evaluate returns, or connect to live execution. It evaluates governance, evidence quality, approval controls, reproducibility, model use, data handling, and operational risk.

## Ideal Buyer

Head of Trading or Risk at a 5–20 seat FX/CFD desk where traders already use general-purpose LLMs and management cannot reconstruct what was asked, what the model answered, or whether the output influenced a decision.

## Commercial Terms

- fixed fee: use the current approved pricing schedule
- fixed duration: 10 business days
- no free pilot
- one desk, one agreed workflow, one final committee presentation
- customer retains all source material
- TitanPilot receives only the minimum data required
- no trading credentials, API secrets, or customer order authority

1. Current-State AI Workflow Map

## Audit Stages

### Day 0 — Qualification

Confirm:
- at least five professional users or a material AI workflow
- an accountable owner exists
- the desk accepts that the audit may reveal weak or ineffective AI use
- no request for returns guarantees or strategy development

Disqualify when:
- buyer wants signals or autonomous execution
- buyer refuses evidence collection
- legal or reputational risk is unacceptable
- firm is unregulated and seeks an AI marketing badge

### Days 1–2 — Discovery

Interview:
- Head of Trading
- one risk/compliance representative
- two representative traders or analysts
- technology owner if one exists

Collect:
- policy documents
- current AI tools and accounts
- example prompts and outputs, sanitized
- decision and approval workflow
- incident examples
- data classification rules
- provider spend and access controls

### Days 3–4 — Workflow Mapping

Map each material AI-assisted decision:

input → model/prompt → output → validation → human review → decision → execution or refusal → retained evidence

Mark every point where evidence is lost or responsibility becomes ambiguous.

### Days 5–6 — Control Testing

Run controlled tests:
- fabricated citation
- malformed output
- conflicting model advice
- stale input
- provider outage
- budget threshold
- approval ambiguity
- inability to reconstruct a historical decision

No test may touch live orders or customer funds.

### Days 7–8 — Gap Scoring

Score each domain 0–5:
- ownership
- input provenance
- output validation
- deterministic boundaries
- human approval
- budget control
- audit trail
- replayability
- incident response
- data security
- provider resilience
- operational monitoring

A score of 0 means absent; 5 means controlled, evidenced, tested, and repeatable.

### Days 9–10 — Report and Committee Readout

Deliver:
- executive summary
- heat map
- top five risks
- immediate containment actions
- 30/60/90-day plan
- target-state architecture
- evidence appendix
- explicit limitations

## Interview Questions

- Which AI tools are used in the trading workflow today?
- Can the desk list every user and account?
- What data is pasted into those tools?
- Which outputs are treated as advice versus research?
- Can a past AI-influenced decision be reconstructed exactly?
- Who approves use of a model or prompt version?
- How are hallucinations detected?
- What happens when providers fail?
- Are costs capped before spend?
- Can the committee distinguish model testimony from deterministic calculations?

## Evidence Requirements

Every finding must contain:
- observed fact
- source
- risk
- affected workflow
- severity
- recommended control
- owner
- due date
- validation method

## Severity

- Critical: credible path to unauthorized trading, secret exposure, or unreconstructable material decisions
- High: ineffective approval, validation, access, or incident control
- Medium: manual or inconsistent governance with limited immediate exposure
- Low: efficiency, documentation, or maturity improvement

## Conversion Path

The audit may convert to a Design Partner or TITAN Desk subscription only when:
- the buyer agrees with the target-state problem
- there is a named owner
- data and deployment boundaries are acceptable
- the customer wants ongoing decision evidence, not a one-off policy document

No pressure-based conversion. The report must remain useful even when the buyer does not purchase software.
