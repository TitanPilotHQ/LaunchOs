# Founder Sales Playbook

## Objective

Win the first five design partners without diluting Titan Pilot into a returns product or free consulting engagement.

## Ideal Customer Profile

Required:

- 5–20 seat proprietary FX/CFD trading desk
- traders already use ChatGPT, Claude, or internal LLM workflows
- Head of Trading can sponsor a pilot
- risk committee, allocator, regulator, or formal governance obligation exists
- willingness to begin in shadow/read-only mode

Disqualify:

- retail signal seekers
- firms demanding guaranteed returns
- HFT/latency-sensitive shops
- gray-market brokers seeking an “AI” badge
- free-pilot requests without decision authority
- firms unwilling to preserve a complete audit record

## Offer Ladder

### Entry Offer — AI Desk Audit

Two-week paid engagement.

Deliverables:

- current AI-usage inventory
- decision-governance gap analysis
- risk and control map
- sample TITAN evidence record
- committee-ready remediation report
- design-partner recommendation

Price hypothesis: USD 7,500 fixed.

### Design Partner

Six-month founder-operated program.

Deliverables:

- single-tenant shadow deployment
- configured symbol set
- Evidence Explorer access
- governed AI analysis workflow
- monthly governance report
- roadmap input
- named reference rights subject to successful outcome

Price hypothesis: USD 2,500/month for first five partners.

### TITAN Desk

List offer after design-partner validation.

Price hypothesis: USD 4,000/month plus clearly disclosed model usage.

## Named-Account Process

### Stage 1 — Research

For each prospect record:

- company
- jurisdiction
- desk type and approximate size
- Head of Trading
- risk/compliance leader
- existing AI statements or hiring signals
- technology stack clues
- known broker/execution environment
- why now
- warm-introduction path

Do not contact until there is one specific reason the account fits.

### Stage 2 — Initial Outreach

Lead with the problem, not the platform.

Template:

> Your traders are probably already using AI in the investment process. The hard question is not whether they use it—it is whether your desk can reconstruct what the model saw, what it claimed, who approved it, and what controls applied. We built a supervised decision record for exactly that problem. I can show you a real AI thesis being challenged, machine-validated, and deliberately refused in 20 minutes.

CTA: a 20-minute evidence walkthrough, not “book a demo.”

### Stage 3 — Discovery

Required questions:

1. Where is AI currently used in research or trade decisions?
2. Is usage sanctioned, tolerated, or hidden?
3. What evidence is retained today?
4. Who asks governance questions—risk, compliance, allocators, regulators?
5. What happens when an AI recommendation is wrong?
6. Can the desk reconstruct who saw and approved it?
7. Are model costs governed or merely reviewed afterward?
8. Which systems would a shadow deployment need to read?
9. What would make a two-week audit worth paying for?
10. Who must approve the engagement?

Do not pitch until at least one costly pain is confirmed.

### Stage 4 — Qualification

Score each 0–2:

- active AI usage
- governance pressure
- executive sponsor
- technical feasibility
- paid-pilot willingness
- reference potential

Minimum: 8/12 to progress.

### Stage 5 — Evidence Demo

Use `09-demo/ENTERPRISE_DEMO_PLAYBOOK.md`.

Never lead with architecture. Lead with one decision record.

### Stage 6 — Paid Audit Proposal

Proposal must include:

- current-state hypothesis
- audit scope
- data boundaries
- no execution authority
- deliverables
- schedule
- customer responsibilities
- fixed price
- conversion path to Design Partner
- confidentiality and data-retention terms

### Stage 7 — Commercial Close

Red lines:

- no free pilot
- no returns-linked pricing
- no unrestricted custom development
- no public logo without written approval
- no production execution in first engagement
- no vague success criteria

## Pipeline Stages

1. Named
2. Researched
3. Contacted
4. Discovery booked
5. Qualified
6. Evidence demo
7. Audit proposed
8. Audit contracted
9. Audit delivered
10. Design partner proposed
11. Closed won/lost

## Weekly Founder Rhythm

Monday:

- select 10 accounts
- complete research
- request warm introductions

Tuesday–Wednesday:

- send personalized outreach
- run discovery and demos

Thursday:

- proposals and follow-ups
- objection analysis

Friday:

- update pipeline
- record lost reasons
- improve assets
- publish one evidence-led insight

## Metrics

Track weekly:

- researched accounts
- personalized contacts
- positive replies
- discovery meetings
- qualification rate
- demos
- paid audits proposed
- audits won
- design partners won
- average days per stage
- top objection
- lost reason

Do not optimize vanity metrics such as impressions before qualified conversations exist.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Day 0: meeting summary with agreed pain and evidence link.

Day 3: relevant artifact—security summary, replay proof, or governance checklist.

Day 7: direct next-step question.

Day 14: close-loop message.

Day 30+: only follow up when there is genuinely new evidence.

## Lost-Deal Taxonomy

- no urgent governance pain
- wrong buyer
- no budget
- wants returns product
- integration too early
- regulatory concern
- founder/company risk
- timing
- competitor/internal build
- no decision

Every loss must have one primary reason. “Went quiet” is not a reason.

## Definition of Done — First Five Partners

- five paid six-month agreements
- at least three active deployments
- two referenceable customers
- one risk/compliance testimonial
- one documented conversion from audit to subscription
- services below 30% of contracted recurring value
- no returns claims introduced to close deals
