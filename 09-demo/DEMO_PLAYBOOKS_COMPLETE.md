# Titan Pilot Demo Playbooks

## Universal rules
Use only real sanitized historical decisions. Show a refusal before an approval. Never show fake live numbers or imply performance. Keep the ask explicit.

## 15-minute executive demo
1. 0–2: The problem — AI is already on the desk without a decision record.
2. 2–7: Evidence Explorer — cited thesis and machine verdict.
3. 7–10: Devil's Advocate veto or caught fabrication.
4. 10–12: Replay proof and budget ledger.
5. 12–15: AI Desk Audit offer and next date.

## 30-minute buyer demo
Add:
- Current governance gap discovery.
- Approval Center safety flow.
- Human/operator provenance.
- Security and single-tenant posture.
- Q&A using the objection library.

## 60-minute technical/risk demo
Add:
- Decision schema and hashes.
- Prompt/version governance.
- Replay and integrity checks.
- Provider failover and budget refusal.
- Architecture boundaries.
- Deployment/data-residency discussion.
- Audit deliverables and implementation scope.

## Demo assets
One caught citation error, one fatal veto, one direction=none cycle, one scored skip, one replay run, one budget reconciliation, security summary, DR measurement.

## Objection responses
- Where is alpha? Not sold; the product governs whatever judgment the desk chooses to use.
- Why pay for shadow? The record and governance are valuable before execution.
- Will traders resist? The record protects the trader as well as the firm.
- What if Titan disappears? Single-tenant deployment, open schema, export, and enterprise escrow.

## Post-demo
Send a one-page recap within 24 hours: stated pain, evidence shown, unanswered questions, proposed audit scope, price, dates, owners.

## Definition of done
The prospect can explain Titan correctly, acknowledges a current governance gap, and accepts or rejects a dated paid next step.
