# Titan Pilot Customer Success OS

## Objective
Move a qualified desk from ungoverned AI usage to a committee-defensible supervised workflow without introducing execution liability prematurely.

## Onboarding phases
1. **Boundary review:** users, models, data, symbols, jurisdictions, retention, execution scope.
2. **Baseline audit:** current AI workflows, controls, spend, records, incidents, DDQ requirements.
3. **Deployment:** single-tenant environment, identity, read-only data, health and backup proof.
4. **Shadow activation:** collect evidence without execution changes.
5. **Governance workshop:** review real decisions, rejections, vetoes, skips, and costs.
6. **Readiness review:** decide whether to remain shadow or enter approved copilot scope.

## Customer health score
- Data freshness and cycle coverage.
- Validation success rate.
- Evidence Explorer usage.
- Committee/risk participation.
- Open incidents and age.
- Audit action completion.
- Champion strength.
- Renewal and payment risk.

## Cadence
- Weekly for first four weeks.
- Monthly operational review.
- Quarterly governance review.
- Annual certification/controls refresh.

## Support priorities
P0 integrity or security; P1 unavailable evidence/control; P2 degraded workflow; P3 guidance/enhancement. Never silently change prompt, scoring, or policy behavior to resolve support pressure.

## Renewal package
Annual decision volume, control activity, validation statistics, incidents, prompt/model changes, committee usage, roadmap fit, and a refreshed governance report.

## Expansion triggers
Additional desks, symbols, jurisdictions, retention, SSO, HA, allocator portal, OEM needs.

## Definition of customer success
The customer can answer, with evidence: what AI said, what it cited, who challenged it, what deterministic controls decided, who approved/refused, what it cost, and what happened.
