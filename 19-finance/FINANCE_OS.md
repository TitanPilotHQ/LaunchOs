# Titan Pilot Finance OS

## Core metrics
Cash, runway, MRR, ARR, gross margin, services mix, burn multiple, CAC, payback, logo retention, NRR, customer concentration, model spend per desk, infrastructure cost per instance.

## Monthly close
Reconcile bank, cards, invoices, payroll/contractors, taxes, deferred revenue, model/provider spend, infrastructure, and receivables. Publish a management pack with actual vs plan and a short variance narrative.

## Planning cases
- Base: profitable wedge with founder-led sales.
- Downside: regulation and category adoption delayed.
- Upside: design partners convert and an OEM channel emerges.

## Spending controls
Every recurring vendor has an owner, purpose, renewal date, budget, security classification, and cancellation procedure. Material discretionary spending requires a written objective and success metric.

## Revenue quality
Track recurring vs services revenue, prepaid terms, discounting, implementation burden, payment delays, and referenceability. Services should decline as a share of total revenue over time.

## Unit economics
Measure gross margin after LLM, hosting, support, deployment, and audit delivery. Do not hide founder labor when evaluating repeatability.

## Fundraising rule
Raise only for named accelerants: engineering capacity, attestation/certification, compliance-oriented GTM, or jurisdictional counsel. Do not raise to avoid pricing or customer discovery.

## Definition of done
Financial reporting is complete when every balance reconciles, assumptions are labeled, downside runway is explicit, and each material variance has an owner and action.
