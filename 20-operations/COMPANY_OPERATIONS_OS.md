# Titan Pilot Company Operations OS

## Operating principles
Evidence over status theater. One accountable owner per outcome. Decisions record tradeoffs and review dates. Bad news travels immediately.

## Weekly rhythm
- Monday: company metrics, cash, pipeline, customer health.
- Tuesday: product and engineering dependencies.
- Wednesday: customer/audit delivery.
- Thursday: research and public claims.
- Friday: finance, risk, incidents, decisions, next-week commitments.

## Decision record
Every material decision records: problem, evidence, alternatives, decision, owner, date, reversibility, affected repositories, risks, review trigger.

## Risk register
Track product, technical, security, regulatory, commercial, people, vendor, and reputation risks with likelihood, impact, controls, owner, next review.

## Incident management
1. Preserve facts and evidence.
2. Stop unsafe activity.
3. Assign incident commander.
4. Communicate known/unknown separately.
5. Correct public claims if affected.
6. Produce blameless postmortem and tracked actions.

## Vendor register
Purpose, owner, data access, cost, renewal, contract, security review, backup/fallback, and exit procedure.

## Document governance
Titan owns engineering truth. Research owns empirical evidence. LaunchOS owns company policy/playbooks. Portfolio owns public presentation. Cross-repo summaries link to the source instead of duplicating it.

## Quarterly review
Strategy, ICP, pricing, category language, roadmap, runway, concentration, regulatory posture, hiring thresholds, and kill criteria.

## Definition of done
An operating process is complete only when owner, cadence, inputs, outputs, metrics, exception path, and evidence location are explicit.
