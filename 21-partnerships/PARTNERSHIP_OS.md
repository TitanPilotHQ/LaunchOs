# Titan Pilot Partnership OS

## Priority partner classes
Prime brokers, execution platforms, market-data vendors, compliance advisers, AI model providers, allocators, and professional-trading communities.

## Partnership evaluation
Score distribution reach, buyer credibility, technical fit, data access, neutrality, implementation burden, gross margin, exclusivity cost, and brand risk.

## Standard process
1. Define shared customer problem.
2. Identify economic buyer and end user.
3. Document data and security boundaries.
4. Agree a 90-day pilot with measurable exit criteria.
5. Assign technical and commercial owners.
6. Decide evidence and publicity rights before work begins.
7. Review continuation, expansion, or termination.

## Red lines
No returns co-marketing. No resale of customer decision data. No removable TITAN evidence attribution in OEM deals. No exclusivity without guaranteed economics. No partner access to secrets or private customer records beyond agreed purpose.

## Partner packages
- Referral: qualified introductions with transparent terms.
- Integration: technical interoperability without resale.
- OEM: embedded evidence pipeline with TITAN attribution.
- Research: jointly published evidence with independent methodology.
- Certification: conformance testing with revocable status.

## KPIs
Qualified pipeline, influenced ARR, implementation time, partner-sourced conversion, support burden, margin, reference value, and strategic learning.

## Definition of done
A partnership exists only when the customer value, economics, boundaries, owners, timeline, evidence rights, and exit path are written.
