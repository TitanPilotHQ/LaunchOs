# Titan Pilot Competition Intelligence

## Competitor classes
- Charting and technical-analysis platforms.
- AI signal vendors.
- Strategy builders and backtest platforms.
- Execution and broker platforms.
- Institutional terminals and data vendors.
- Enterprise AI-governance suites.
- In-house browser and spreadsheet workflows.

## Evaluation axes
Decision provenance, evidence citations, machine validation, adversarial review, deterministic scoring, human approval, replay, budget control, model neutrality, broker neutrality, exportability, security, and audit usability.

## Core distinction
Competitors usually show the market, a rule, or a signal. Titan Pilot shows a decision that can be cross-examined.

## Battlecard structure
For every competitor record:
- Primary buyer and job.
- Current positioning and pricing.
- What it does better.
- What Titan does differently.
- Evidence for every claim.
- Likely objection.
- Recommended response.
- Last verified date.

## Named monitoring set
TradingView, TrendSpider, Capitalise.ai, Tickeron, Composer, LuxAlgo, MetaTrader, Bloomberg, LSEG, Devexperts, ION, model-risk and AI-governance vendors.

## Rules
Never publish an unverified feature comparison. Concede strengths. Avoid declaring a competitor unable to build something; state architectural or business-model friction with evidence. Separate direct competitors from substitutes and future acquirers.

## Review cadence
Monthly light scan, quarterly full matrix, event-driven review after major launch, acquisition, regulation, pricing change, or public incident.

## Definition of done
A competitive claim is usable only when dated, sourced, scoped, and translated into a buyer-relevant consequence.
