# 100-Desk Prospecting System

## Objective

Build a qualified list of 100 professional trading desks that match the first-customer thesis. This is not a scraped mailing list. Each account must have a reason to belong, a likely buyer, evidence of AI adoption or governance pressure, and a defensible outreach angle.

## Geographic Order

1. London
2. Dubai / DIFC
3. Limassol / Cyprus
4. Sydney / Melbourne
5. selected EU financial centers

The order reflects concentration of FX/CFD firms, oversight, and founder-accessibility—not market size alone.

## Qualification Score

Score 0–2 in each category:

- Desk size: 5–20 seats ideal
- Asset fit: FX/CFD or adjacent discretionary trading
- AI signal: public evidence of AI experimentation, automation, or model hiring
- Governance pressure: regulated jurisdiction, allocator reporting, committee structure
- Buyer accessibility: named Head of Trading, COO, CRO, CTO, or founder
- Build-vs-buy: small enough not to maintain a full internal governance platform
- Reputation: acceptable counterparty and no gray-market warning signs

Maximum 14. Prioritize 10+. Do not contact below 7 without a specific strategic reason.

## Required Account Record

- account name
- legal entity and jurisdiction
- website
- desk type
- estimated seats
- asset classes
- regulatory status
- likely buyer and role
- secondary stakeholders
- public AI evidence
- current tooling clues
- governance trigger
- likely objection
- tailored opening hypothesis
- qualification score
- source links and dates
- outreach status
- next action

## Research Sources

Use public, legal sources only:
- official company websites
- regulator registers
- executive profiles
- conference agendas
- job postings
- engineering posts
- interviews and podcasts
- public vendor case studies
- corporate filings where available

Never use leaked data, private contact databases without lawful access, or inferred personal details that are not professionally relevant.

## Segments

### A — Immediate Audit Candidates

Already use AI or automation publicly, face oversight, and have a reachable decision-maker.

Action: direct AI Desk Audit outreach.

### B — Design Partner Candidates

Strong problem fit and technical appetite, but need category education.

Action: evidence-led founder conversation and live demo.

### C — Channel or Strategic Accounts

Prime brokers, execution platforms, technology vendors, allocators, and consultants who can introduce multiple desks.

Action: partnership conversation, not standard product pitch.

### D — Monitor

Good future fit but too large, too early, inaccessible, or unclear.

Action: track trigger events; no repeated cold outreach.

## Outreach Sequence

### Touch 1 — Specific Risk Hypothesis

Lead with the desk's likely governance problem, not TITAN features.

Structure:
1. observed public fact
2. implication for AI-assisted trading governance
3. one concrete question
4. link to one relevant evidence artifact
5. request for a short diagnostic conversation

### Touch 2 — Receipt

Share a concise, real artifact: a caught citation failure, veto record, replay proof, or decision-evidence example.

### Touch 3 — Audit Offer

Offer the fixed-scope AI Desk Audit. State price and boundaries. No free pilot.

### Touch 4 — Close the Loop

Politely close the sequence and state the trigger for reconnecting.

## CRM Stages

Researching → Qualified → Contacted → Replied → Discovery → Audit Proposed → Audit Won → Design Partner Proposed → Customer → Nurture → Disqualified

Every stage requires a dated next action. No opportunity may remain open without one.

## Weekly Metrics

- newly researched accounts
- accounts scoring 10+
- decision-makers identified
- first touches sent
- positive reply rate
- discovery calls
- audits proposed
- audits won
- disqualification reasons
- days in stage

Do not optimize raw email volume. Optimize qualified conversations and paid audits.

## Quality Gate

The first 100-desk list is complete when:
- all 100 have source-backed records
- at least 60 score 10+
- at least 80 have a named likely buyer
- no prohibited or reputationally unsafe account remains
- every outreach message has an account-specific hypothesis
- the list has been manually reviewed before activation.
