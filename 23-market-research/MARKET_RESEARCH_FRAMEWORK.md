# Titan Pilot Market Research Framework

## Core research questions
Who uses AI in trading today? Which decisions does it influence? What evidence is retained? Who approves or overrides it? What does the risk committee ask? What pain creates willingness to pay?

## Methods
- Structured buyer interviews.
- Workflow observation.
- DDQ and policy document collection.
- Regulatory and enforcement review.
- Willingness-to-pay experiments.
- Win/loss interviews.
- Incident and loss postmortems.
- Competitive product evaluation.

## Interview metadata
Role, jurisdiction, desk size, asset class, business model, existing tools, AI usage maturity, committee structure, buying authority, and confidentiality level.

## Evidence discipline
Separate verbatim observation, measured fact, third-party source, inference, and hypothesis. Record confidence and date. Never convert one buyer opinion into a market claim.

## Research outputs
ICP revisions, problem ranking, objection frequency, category language, pricing evidence, integration priorities, legal constraints, market-size assumptions, and roadmap implications.

## Sample interview guide
1. Walk through the last time AI influenced a trade.
2. What record exists?
3. What would an auditor or allocator see?
4. What control would you never delegate?
5. What failure worries you most?
6. What would make a paid audit worthwhile?
7. Who signs and who can block purchase?

## Repository flow
Raw confidential notes remain private. Sanitized findings enter LaunchOS. Publishable aggregate evidence enters Research. Public copy in Portfolio may use only approved claims.

## Definition of done
A research conclusion is actionable only when its sample, evidence type, confidence, contrary evidence, and product/commercial consequence are explicit.
