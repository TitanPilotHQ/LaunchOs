# Titan Pilot Hiring OS

## Hiring sequence
1. Founding engineer when product delivery constrains revenue.
2. Deployment and compliance engineer when the AI Desk Audit becomes repeatable.
3. GTM lead after ten paying customers and a documented founder-sales motion.

## Role scorecard
Every role defines its mission, twelve-month outcomes, competencies, anti-patterns, decision rights, collaboration map, and thirty-sixty-ninety-day evidence.

## Interview system
Use a structured screen, a real work sample based on a sanitized company artifact, a written tradeoff memo, and reference checks tied to claimed outcomes.

## Engineering evaluation
The candidate must identify a real risk, distinguish an invariant from a preference, propose a reversible plan, and explain how completion will be proven.

## Commercial evaluation
The candidate must qualify an account, handle the alpha objection without hype, and establish a dated next step.

## Cultural invariants
No hidden bad news. No unsupported claims. No silent weakening of safety or auditability. No complexity without a measured need. Default to written evidence.

## Onboarding
Cover the company thesis, repository ownership, product demo, engineering handbook, commercial strategy, current metrics, decision log, and a first thirty-day outcome.

## Definition of done
A hire succeeds when the scorecard outcome is achieved without degrading the company's evidence, safety, or honesty standards.
