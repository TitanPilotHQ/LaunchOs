# Titan Pilot Investor Deck v1

## Deck Goal

Explain Titan Pilot as a serious AI infrastructure company for financial markets, with a defensible engineering moat and disciplined path to autonomy.

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Titan Pilot**

AI Decision Infrastructure for Financial Markets

Autonomous intelligence. Engineered for markets.

## Slide 2 — The Problem

Financial markets are increasingly automated, but most automation is still opaque, brittle, and difficult to audit.

AI makes the problem bigger if it is allowed to act without boundaries.

## Slide 3 — Why Now

- LLMs can reason over structured market context
- Financial infrastructure needs explainability
- Traders want automation without losing control
- AI systems require audit trails and safety gates
- Cloud infrastructure makes resilient autonomous systems practical

## Slide 4 — Existing Solutions Fall Short

### Manual Trading
Flexible but inconsistent and emotional.

### Trading Bots
Fast but opaque and fragile.

### AI Signal Tools
Promising but often unconstrained and non-replayable.

## Slide 5 — The Titan Pilot Answer

Titan Pilot separates reasoning from authority.

AI produces structured analysis.

Deterministic software controls scoring, risk, execution, reconciliation, and recovery.

## Slide 6 — Product Architecture

Market Context → Dossier → AI Analyst → Devil's Advocate → Deterministic Score → Risk Gate → Execution → Broker → Replay

## Slide 7 — Trust Architecture

- immutable event log

- broker reconciliation

- continuous health checks
- verified backups
- operational alerts

## Slide 8 — What Makes It Defensible

The model is not the moat.

- deterministic scoring
- broker truth reconciliation
- operational hardening
- evaluation discipline
